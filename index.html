About Me (boring!)

Who?

I am a curious creature with an artist's disposition. Love saved me from conceit and (eventually) taught me gratitude. I'm not especially ambitious, but I would very much like, one day, to shine as brightly as those who've guided me in my life. Despite all that, I still somehow frequently struggle to connect with other people on a personal level. Go figure. No doubt, there's some superficial--but persistent!--idiosyncrasy in my unconscious protocol for percieving and analyzing my experiences and the world, more broadly, underlying this flummoxing obscurity. Never mind that, though; it's nothing new (and, perhaps, a bit too wordy). Indeed, you've met me before in one flavor or another. So, don't be fooled into thinking that I'm someone unfamiliar--no matter how hard I may try to trick you!

What?

Yeah, I might not've paid any attention either. Anyways, I've been known to teach: mostly math (calculus, engineering differential equations, linear algebra, and middle/high-school topics) and music (piano, guitar, drums, and music-theory). I've also played music semi-professionally, including as a founding member of Vehicle Collector, and professionally, including as a founding member of Smacktalk and as an occasional session player. Otherwise, you're most likely to find me doing math, playing my piano, or tinkering on my computer.

When?

1997 onwards: 24/7, baby! (Pacific Standard Time)

Where?

Probably Mercer Island, WA. Also, (frequently) Discord and (rarely) Facebook.

Why?

Good question... Camus described an invincible summer, Buddha spoke of nirvana, and Nirvana sang about (teen) spirit. Kidding aside, there is a certain visceral feeling, similar to focus or drive, which seems to be most saliently experienced by almost everybody (at least initially) in a fleeting kind of way while being pulled away from something meaningful by something measurably less meaningful. This feeling is stripped of its ostensibly intrinsically transient nature--it is distilled, foritified, and elaborated--by exercising deliberate, controlled experiential dissociation. For Buddha, this was meditation--a relatively pure and direct approach, to be sure. Writing took that role for Camus. Likewise for many of the great Xers and Yists, X and Y functioned so. In fact, the thing is not actually important. What is important is the realization that this feeling is not really about any one thing in particular: it is the emotional essence of I'd like to call "meaning," and can be cultivated independently of life-circumstance. An ember inside each of us, it requires only oxygen, sustinance, and shelter to eventually burn brightly. "Why," you ask? So that my fire may flourish.
